AppSec Cali is packed with great talks by an international lineup of great speakers, top level all-day training classes, lots of prize giveaways, and parties during its 4 days at the beach in Santa Monica, California.

Ask yourself: “Do I want to go to the office, or would I rather go to the beach for two, three, or four days?“ If you live in the north, would you rather dig your car out of snow/ice on a few fine January mornings, or go to the beach? Go ahead, make your day, and ours, too!

Here are all the details you may need to effectively and efficiently navigate the training workshops and the conference!

  • Just an important reminder to everyone: Tuesday and Wednesday are training days with a separate fee; unless you are taking training, the conference begins on Thursday.
  • Please read the whole page, it will answer many of the questions you have about the logistics of the conference.

Code of Conduct

AppSec California is dedicated to providing a harassment-free conference experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion, or political affiliation. We have a zero-tolerance policy for harassment in any form. Violations by any registered attendee or participant at AppSec California may result in expulsion and possible ban from future OWASP events.


Weather and Attire

  • Sunny 73 – 80°F daytime, partly cloudy
  • 0% Precipitation!
  • 55 – 58°F evening.
  • Bring a jacket (we are adjacent to the beach), swimsuit for the brave, and wear long pants.

Parking

  • Parking lots do not open until 7 A.M.
  • Thursday and Friday (Conference)
  • Look for a volunteer who will be handing out the limited number of free daily parking passes to attendees, speakers, and volunteers. If you don’t get a free one, YOU MUST purchase the $8.00 parking pass each day! The parking pass machine is just outside the registration area.The city police ticket parked cars that don’t have the daily pass.
  • Tuesday and Wednesday (Training Classes ONLY)
  • If you are a student in a training class, YOU MUST purchase the $8.00 parking pass each day! The parking pass machine is just outside the registration area. The city police ticket parked cars that don’t have the daily pass. Volunteers and Instructors, please pick up your parking passes at registration.
  • Overflow Parking is available at any of the city lots within about 1 block of the Annenberg; get a parking pass first.

Directions

From Los Angeles: Take the 10 West, which will become Pacific Coast Highway when it reaches the beach. At the second traffic light, Beach House Way, turn left into the parking lot.

From Malibu: Take Pacific Coast Highway south. Turn right into the parking lot at Beach House Way.

From Santa Monica (downtown): Head west towards the Pacific Ocean. Turn left onto Ocean Ave. Turn right at Moomat Ahiko Way (after Colorado/pier entrance). Look for signs for the Pacific Coast Highway (PCH). Turn left into the parking lot at Beach House Way.

Consider Lyft or Uber. Rides from downtown Santa Monica are under $10.

Just a reminder

AppSec California is organized and run by a 100% volunteer staff that comes from all over California to put on the conference. Please stop any of us and tell us what you think. We hope it is all good feedback, but we also want to know if we can improve the conference for you next year. Please also remember to fill out the post-conference survey with your feedback, and leave your name for a chance to win $100 gift cards. If you don’t tell what you like or dislike we will never know.


Dietary Restrictions

or those of you who have special dietary restrictions that we need to accommodate during the breakfasts or lunches at AppSec Cali, you need to let us know ASAP.

Email us at : AppSec-California-info@OWASP.ORG

Please let us know what the restrictions are, be as specific as possible; e.g. no peanuts, 100% vegan, fruitarian…. Also, tell us what days you plan on attending, so the catering group and plan accordingly: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday and Friday.
